## [First complete Herculaneum scroll read using AI](https://scrollprize.org/firstscroll) ⭐️ 9.0/10

For the first time, an entire Herculaneum scroll has been read using AI and advanced X-ray imaging, unlocking ancient Greek text preserved by the Vesuvius eruption. The achievement was announced by the Vesuvius Challenge team, which awarded $1,800,500 in prizes for the breakthrough. This breakthrough demonstrates that AI can non-destructively read carbonized papyri, potentially recovering hundreds of lost classical texts from the only surviving ancient library. It opens a new era in digital humanities and could transform our understanding of ancient philosophy and literature. The scroll was scanned using X-ray micro-CT at Diamond Light Source, and machine learning algorithms detected ink by identifying subtle differences in papyrus fibers. The ink in this scroll appears more clearly in X-rays, possibly due to lead content, making it easier for AI to decipher.

hackernews · verditelabs · Jun 25, 15:48 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48675179)

**Background**: The Herculaneum papyri are over 1,800 carbonized scrolls discovered in the 18th century at the Villa of the Papyri, buried by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD. They contain Greek philosophical texts, mostly by Epicurean philosopher Philodemus, and are the only surviving library from antiquity in its entirety. Traditional unrolling risks destroying the scrolls, so non-invasive techniques like X-ray imaging and AI are essential.
